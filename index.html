About

Bridging power engineering, cloud, and AI.

Song Zhang (IEEE S'13 M'14 SM'20) is a recognized leader at the intersection of power systems, cloud technology, and artificial intelligence. He received his Ph.D. from Arizona State University in 2014 and is currently a Senior Solution Architect and Grid Solution Leader at Amazon Web Services, Energy and Utilities IBU.

With one and a half decade of experience spanning utility operations, R&D, and cloud architecture, Song has pioneered the convergence of AI, cloud-native platforms, and power grid modernization — driving measurable transformation across the energy sector. His work has directly influenced how utilities adopt cloud and AI to improve reliability, reduce costs, and accelerate the clean energy transition.

Before AWS, Song served as R&D Lead Analyst and Cloud CoE Lead at ISO New England, where he led cloud adoption initiatives and owned cloud application and infrastructure design for large-scale planning studies. Earlier, he worked at Alstom Grid (now a GE Vernova company) from 2014 to 2017, contributing to energy management and grid automation technologies.

His research spans power system optimal control, operations, stability and dynamics, alongside disruptive technologies — generative/agentic AI, cloud-native data engineering architecture, high performance computing, digital twins and Internet of Things — applied to smart grid challenges. He has published 30+ peer-reviewed papers with 1,300+ citations, holds a patent, and has received multiple IEEE PES Best Paper Awards and ISO New England CEO Innovation Awards.
